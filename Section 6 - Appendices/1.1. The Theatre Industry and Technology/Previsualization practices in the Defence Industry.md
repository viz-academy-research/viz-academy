### Introduction to Previsualization
Previsualization, or "previs," is the practice of visualizing and simulating scenarios before they happen in real life. In the defence industry, it helps military personnel and planners prepare for operations by creating virtual environments that mimic real-world conditions. This approach is crucial for training soldiers, testing strategies, and optimizing equipment maintenance, all while reducing risks and costs.

### Tools and Technologies
Several companies provide specialized tools for defence previsualization. For example:
- **Bohemia Interactive Simulations** offers VBS4, used in over 60 countries for realistic combat and terrain simulations, aiding in mechanized infantry training ([Bohemia Interactive Simulations](https://bisimulations.com/products/)).
- **AnyLogic** provides software for modelling complex systems, such as optimizing aircraft maintenance schedules, enhancing operational readiness ([AnyLogic](https://www.anylogic.com/defense/)).
- Relevant to this study, Finnish-US firm Younite is beginning to provide [photorealistic digital twin services](https://younite.ai/defense) to the defence industry.

These tools create detailed virtual scenarios, allowing for safe and effective preparation.

### Applications and Examples
Previsualization is applied in various areas, including:
- Combat simulations for urban operations and counter-insurgency, helping soldiers practice decision-making.

### Survey Note: Comprehensive Analysis of Previsualization Practices in the Defence Industry

This note provides an in-depth exploration of previsualization practices within the defence industry, expanding on the key points and applications outlined above. It aims to offer a detailed, professional overview suitable for stakeholders, researchers, and practitioners, drawing from a range of sources to ensure a comprehensive understanding.

#### 1. Definition and Importance
Previsualization, often termed "previs," refers to the use of digital simulation and visualization tools to create virtual representations of scenarios before their real-world execution. In the defence sector, this practice is pivotal for enhancing training, strategic planning, and operational readiness. By simulating complex environments, defence organizations can mitigate risks, reduce costs associated with physical training, and improve decision-making under realistic conditions. The importance lies in its ability to replicate battlefield dynamics, equipment functionality, and disaster response scenarios, ensuring personnel are well-prepared for diverse challenges.

#### 2. Key Tools and Technologies
The defence industry relies on advanced simulation software to facilitate previsualization, with several notable providers:

- **Bohemia Interactive Simulations (BISim)**:
  - BISim is a global leader in simulation software, with products used by over 300 integrators and hundreds of thousands of military personnel annually across more than 60 countries. Their flagship product, VBS4, is designed specifically for military training and simulation, offering high-fidelity visualization capabilities. It supports the creation of geo-specific environments using satellite imagery and drone-captured data, enabling rapid development of training scenarios. For instance, VBS4 is used for mechanized infantry training, integrating AI-assisted command systems and terrain-based strategies to enhance combat readiness ([Bohemia Interactive Simulations](https://bisimulations.com/products/)).
  - BISim also offers visualization products for desktop, VR, and multi-channel configurations, as well as software development kits (SDKs) for customizing applications, ensuring flexibility for diverse defence needs.

- **AnyLogic**:
  - AnyLogic provides multimethod simulation software, combining system dynamics, agent-based modeling, and discrete event simulation, tailored for defence applications. It is used for modelling complex processes such as military aircraft maintenance scheduling and disaster response planning. A notable case study involves optimizing maintenance for Apache Attack Helicopters, reducing turnaround times from 35 hours through virtual testing ([AnyLogic](https://www.anylogic.com/defense/)). This capability allows defence facilities to evaluate the impact of new strategies, such as automated transportation systems, without physical deployment.
  - The software supports the creation of digital twins, providing near real-time analysis of operations within production lines and military bases, enhancing decision-making with detailed 3D visualizations and statistics.

Other providers, such as STE Antycip and VirTra, also contribute to the landscape. STE Antycip offers solutions for terrain modeling and cyber defence training, while VirTra provides combat simulators with realistic scenarios for marksmanship training, emphasizing ballistically accurate environments.

#### 3. Specific Applications
Previsualization in defence encompasses a broad spectrum of applications, each tailored to enhance operational effectiveness:

- **Military Training**:
  - Combat simulations are a cornerstone, enabling soldiers to practice in virtual environments that replicate urban terrain operations (Military Operations in Urban Terrain or MOUT), counter-insurgency, and counter-terrorism scenarios. Tools like VBS4 allow for the simulation of realistic combat conditions, including interactions with civilians, non-combatants, and opposing forces, validating soldiers' approach, reaction time, and combat efficiency.
  - Maintenance training is another critical area, where simulations model the maintenance of complex systems such as battle tanks, artillery guns, and fighter jets. For example, AnyLogic's case studies demonstrate its use in optimizing aircraft maintenance, ensuring efficient resource allocation and reducing downtime.

- **Augmented Reality (AR) in Defence**:
  - AR is increasingly integral to previsualization, overlaying digital information onto the real world to enhance training and operational capabilities. A detailed study on ResearchGate highlights AR's applications, including:
    - Combat medic training, where AR simulates casualty scenarios, providing real-time medical instructions to extend the "golden hour" for treatment, crucial for battlefield medical services ([ResearchGate: Augmented Reality in Military Applications](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/350398483_Augmented_Reality_in_Military_Applications)).
    - Training combat engineers (sappers, miners) by visualizing potential hazards like improvised explosive devices (IEDs), improving risk assessment and operational safety.
    - Weapons maintenance, where AR guides technicians through disassembly and repair processes, reducing downtime for systems like fighter jets.
  - AR enhances situational awareness by integrating data on terrain, enemy positions, and environmental factors, reducing collateral damage and improving battlefield perception. The military AR market is projected to reach USD 1.79 billion by 2025, reflecting its growing adoption ([Techristic](https://www.techristic.com/global-military-augmented-reality-mar-ket-estimated-at-1-79-billion-by-2025/)).

- **Strategic Planning and Analysis**:
  - Previsualization tools are used for large-scale strategic planning, such as disaster response and evacuation planning. AnyLogic, for instance, has been applied to simulate metro station vulnerability during emergencies, accounting for crowd behavior, environmental variables, and technology systems, ensuring effective emergency management ([AnyLogic Case Studies](https://www.anylogic.com/resources/case-studies/metro-station-vulnerability-analysis-with-crowd-simulation/)).
  - Simulation software also supports the testing of new technologies, such as autonomous systems for supply transportation, demining, and reconnaissance, allowing defence planners to evaluate performance and integrate constraints virtually.

#### 4. Case Studies and Practical Examples
To illustrate the practical impact of previsualization, consider the following examples:

- **Mechanized Infantry Training with VBS4**:
  - BISim's VBS4 has been utilized for mechanized infantry training, as highlighted in a recent news item from March 14, 2025. The simulation enhances combat readiness by integrating AI-assisted command systems and terrain-based strategies, allowing soldiers to practice in environments that mirror specific operational theatres. This capability was showcased at ITEC 2025, demonstrating the transformation of high-resolution survey data into training environments within hours ([Bohemia Interactive Simulations News](https://bisimulations.com/news/blogs/mechanized-infantry-training)).

- **Aircraft Maintenance Optimization with AnyLogic**:
  - AnyLogic's application in military aircraft maintenance, particularly for Apache Attack Helicopters, reduced turnaround times by optimizing scheduling and staffing. This case study, detailed in their resources, involved simulating the maintenance process to identify bottlenecks, achieving a 35-hour maintenance cycle with improved efficiency ([AnyLogic Case Studies](https://www.anylogic.com/resources/case-studies/military-aircraft-maintenance-scheduling-and-staffing-optimization/)).

- **AR in Combat Medic Training**:
  - AR systems have been deployed to simulate casualty care, as noted in the ResearchGate paper. These systems use AI and machine learning to provide real-time feedback, extending the "golden hour" for treating casualties, a critical factor in battlefield medical operations. This application enhances training outcomes by simulating realistic scenarios with high fidelity ([ResearchGate: Augmented Reality in Military Applications](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/350398483_Augmented_Reality_in_Military_Applications)).

#### 5. Future Trends and Emerging Developments
The field of previsualization in defence is poised for significant growth, driven by technological advancements:

- **Integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI)**:
  - AI is increasingly integrated into simulation tools, enhancing realism and adaptability. For instance, Sentient Digital leverages AI to create lifelike environments for wargames, with adaptive enemy behaviors that respond to user actions, improving training effectiveness. This real-time adaptability is a major strength, preparing personnel for dynamic real-world opponents ([Sentient Digital Blog](https://sdi.ai/blog/military-training-simulation-software-ai/)).

- **Advanced Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR)**:
  - VR and AR systems are becoming more immersive, with applications in parachuting exercises, urban combat simulations, and maintenance training. The AR industry, including VR, is valued at USD 767.67 billion by 2025, with military AR specifically estimated at USD 1.79 billion, indicating robust growth ([Techristic](https://www.techristic.com/global-military-augmented-reality-mar-ket-estimated-at-1-79-billion-by-2025/)).

- **Digital Twins**:
  - Digital twin technology is gaining traction, creating virtual replicas of physical assets like military bases and equipment. Simio, for example, provides tools for creating digital twins that offer near real-time analysis of operations, supporting predictive maintenance and strategic planning ([Simio](https://www.simio.com/applications/military-simulation-software/)).

- **Cyber Defence Simulations**:
  - As cyber threats escalate, previsualization tools are adapted for cyber defence training. BISim offers solutions for simulating cyber attacks in virtual environments, preparing operator and leadership teams for dynamic scenarios, reflecting the multi-domain nature of modern warfare ([Bohemia Interactive Simulations](https://bisimulations.com/products/)).

#### 6. Challenges and Considerations
Despite its benefits, previsualization faces challenges, particularly with AR systems:
- Input methods, such as keyboards and mice, are often inadequate, with proposed solutions like voice commands or Morse Codes via eye blinks to enhance usability.
- Information overload is a concern, necessitating filtering algorithms to manage data effectively.
- Depth perception issues require solutions like two-camera systems with dense depth maps for accurate visualization.
- Hardware costs and weight are critical, especially given the average soldier's combat load of USD 17,500 in 2007, which must not impede combat efficiency ([Military Bases](https://militarybases.com/military-articles/cost-of-military-spending/)).

These challenges highlight the need for cost-affordable, rugged systems to ensure widespread adoption.

#### 7. Market and Historical Context
The defence previsualization market is growing, with AR and VR technologies driving innovation. Historically, AR has been used by fighter pilots for decades via Helmet Mounted Displays, providing missile targeting and aircraft performance data (speed, elevation). The current market projections, such as the USD 1.79 billion estimate for military AR by 2025, underscore its strategic importance ([Techristic](https://www.techristic.com/global-military-augmented-reality-mar-ket-estimated-at-1-79-billion-by-2025/)).

#### 8. Conclusion
Previsualization practices in the defence industry are transforming training, planning, and operations through advanced simulation and visualization tools. From BISim's VBS4 to AnyLogic's multimethod models, and the growing role of AR, these technologies enhance safety, efficiency, and readiness. Future trends, including AI integration and digital twins, promise further advancements, though challenges like hardware costs and information overload require ongoing innovation. This comprehensive approach ensures defence organizations are equipped to meet evolving threats and operational demands.
